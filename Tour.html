NEOM means “new future,” and it is a company with more than 2,800 employees from 86 countries, who currently work and live here in the NEOM region. This confirms that NEOM has indeed become a home for dreamers and achievers, who aspire to contribute to building a new economic model for the Kingdom and the entire world. Wadi Al Disa, one of the sites in the city of Tabuk in Saudi Arabia, is characterized by an abundance of water streams, palm trees and wild herbs, which makes it acquire a unique nature, in addition to being one of the most important natural foundations of the NEOM project. It was named after the village of Al-Disa, which is located at the entrance to the valley from the western side. The word Al Disa means (valley full of palm trees). It is also called “Qaraqar”, or Wadi Damah Jabal al-Lawz is located in the northwest of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, near the Jordanian border in the Tabuk region. The snowfall of Jabal al-Lawz is an opportunity for visitors to go to the high mountains, ski on the snow, and enjoy the natural scenery. It is also possible to camp in the snow using traditional Arab tents. After a long day of playing in the snow, skiing, and practicing various activities, you can camp using the appropriate tools for camping in the snow, with pure white snow accumulating everywhere, and the bright stars of the sky.
HAQL WADI TAYIB ISM PARK UMLIJ
Haql Governorate is currently considered the most important tourist center in the Tabuk region, and Al-Durrah Port represents the last stop in the Kingdom for travelers via land route, passing through the port of Aqaba, and when some travelers need to rest and stock up on the necessary luggage for travel. There are many tourist sites in Haql Governorate that are suitable for various purposes: the sunken ship, Al Wasl Island, and the coastal village of Al Hamidiya in Haql. Wadi “Tayeb Ism” is an elevated area located in Tabuk, northwest of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, at an altitude of approximately 314 metres. It is 253 km away from Tabuk on the banks of the Gulf of Aqaba. It is close to the Al-Bid’a area, which was one of the evidences of successive civilizations in the periods before Christ until the dawn of time. It is the White City, or the Saudi Maldives as some like to call it. On the coast of the Red Sea between the cities of Al-Wajh and Yanbu, lies the city of Umluj, the main important port in the Tabuk region. In ancient times, the city of Umluj was the second largest commercial city after the holy city of Mecca, and it bore the name of Al-Hawraa. There are many traces and remains of ancient ruins evident in the north of the city, and it is believed that the city lived during the Roman era and was known as the White City.
